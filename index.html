Pre-Trip

For guests arriving early · Hotels & transportation

Vietnam is a long way to travel for most of us, so many guests are flying in a few days early to shake off the jet lag and explore before we set sail. If you're arriving a couple of days ahead, here are three wonderful places to base yourself — pick whichever suits your style. Each has an easy route to the ship on embarkation day.

Who's staying where: Andrew & Bree Family & friends Open — no bookings yet
1

Hanoi

The Capital

Dive into Vietnam's buzzing capital — the lantern-lit Old Quarter, unforgettable street food, lakeside cafés, and centuries of history. It's the most popular base, with the easiest international arrival and plenty to fill a few days.

2

Ninh Binh

Ha Long Bay on Land

Trade boats for sampans among towering limestone karsts, emerald rivers, and rice paddies at Tam Coc and Trang An. Quieter and more rural than the city — a breathtaking, peaceful base, and an easy detour on the way to the bay.

3

Ha Long Bay

Right by the Ship

Stay where it all happens — Ha Long City sits right beside the harbor our ship departs from. Wake up to bay views and enjoy the most relaxed embarkation morning of all, with no long transfer on sailing day.

Post-Trip

Extend the adventure · Where we're headed & what to explore

After we disembark on April 2, we're not done with Vietnam yet — and you're warmly invited to join us on either leg, or branch off on your own adventure. Here's where we're headed, plus a roundup of the country's best.

Our Plans — Come Along!

Ha Giang Loop

Ha Giang → Cao Bang · April 2–7

First, we're heading deep into Vietnam's far north on a 5-day private Ha Giang – Cao Bang – Ban Gioc adventure. We'll wind along the legendary Happiness Road over the Ma Pi Leng Pass, boat through the Nho Que River's Tu San Canyon, stand at Lung Cu (Vietnam's northernmost point), and finish at the thundering Ban Gioc Waterfall — staying in local homestays and meeting H'Mong, Tay, and Lo Lo communities along the way. Ride your own motorbike, hop on as a passenger, or go easy-rider with a driver. Keen to join? Let us know — the more the merrier!

🛂 Visa & Passport

Most visitors need a visa for Vietnam — the easiest route is the official E-Visa, applied for online about 2–4 weeks ahead. Bring a passport valid for at least 6 months. We'll need your passport details before the cruise for onboard registration.

✈️ Flights

Fly into Noi Bai International Airport (HAN) in Hanoi. Book early for the best fares — it's a long-haul trip for most of us. Plan to arrive at least a day before the cruise.

🧳 What to Pack

Wedding attire, comfortable clothes for cave & island visits, swimwear, sturdy walking shoes, sunscreen, a light rain layer, and motion-sickness medicine. Bring any personal medications you need.

💵 Money

The currency is the Vietnamese Dong (VND). Cards work in cities; carry cash for markets and small vendors. ATMs are widely available. Onboard, you'll settle drinks, spa, and incidentals at check-out.

📱 Staying Connected

Grab a local eSIM or SIM card for cheap data, and use the Grab app for taxis. Note: Wi-Fi onboard is available in the restaurant but limited out in the bay — a lovely excuse to unplug.

📋 Good to Know

No outside food or drink onboard without the cruise's consent. Smoking only in designated outdoor areas — never in cabins. The full onboard schedule and dress code will be shared before we sail.
