Pre-Trip

For guests arriving early · Hotels & transportation

Vietnam is a long way to travel, so many guests may want to arrive a few days early to settle in, shake off the jet lag, and explore before we set sail.

Below are three great places to base yourself before the cruise. Choose whichever best fits your travel style. Each has a straightforward route to the ship on embarkation day.

1

Hanoi

The Capital

Dive into Vietnam's buzzing capital — the lantern-lit Old Quarter, unforgettable street food, lakeside cafés, and centuries of history. It's the most popular base, with the easiest international arrival and plenty to fill a few days.

2

Ninh Binh

Ha Long Bay on Land

Trade boats for sampans among towering limestone karsts, emerald rivers, and rice paddies at Tam Coc and Trang An. Quieter and more rural than the city — a breathtaking, peaceful base, and an easy detour on the way to the bay.

3

Ha Long Bay

Right by the Ship

Stay where it all happens — Ha Long City sits right beside the harbor our ship departs from. Wake up to bay views and enjoy the most relaxed embarkation morning of all, with no long transfer on sailing day.

🛂 Visa & Passport

Everyone will need a visa for Vietnam. The easiest route is the official e-visa, which is an online application and is usually processed in 3-5 working days. We recommend applying about 2-4 weeks before travel so there is plenty of buffer. Bring a passport valid for at least 6 months. We'll need your passport details before the cruise for onboard registration. Read the U.S. travel guidance

✈️ Flights

Fly into Noi Bai International Airport (HAN) in Hanoi. Book early for the best fares — it's a long-haul trip for most of us. Plan to arrive at least a day before the cruise, though we suggest a few days early to acclimate to the time change and rest.

🧳 What to Pack

Wedding attire, comfortable clothes for cave & island visits, swimwear, sturdy walking shoes, sunscreen, a light rain layer, and motion-sickness medicine (flat water expected). Bring any personal medications you need.

💵 Money

The currency is the Vietnamese Dong (VND). Cards work in cities; carry cash for markets and small vendors. ATMs are widely available. Onboard, you'll settle drinks, spa, and incidentals at check-out.

📱 Staying Connected

Grab a local eSIM or SIM card for cheap data, and use the Grab app for taxis. Note: Wi-Fi onboard is available in the restaurant but limited out in the bay — a lovely excuse to unplug.

📋 Good to Know

No outside food or drink onboard without the cruise's consent. Smoking only in designated outdoor areas — never in cabins. The full onboard schedule and dress code will be shared before we sail.
