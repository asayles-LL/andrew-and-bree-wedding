Travel Essentials

Everything you need to know before you go

🛂 Visa & Passport

Most visitors need a visa for Vietnam — the easiest route is the official E-Visa, applied for online about 2–4 weeks ahead. Bring a passport valid for at least 6 months. We'll need your passport details before the cruise for onboard registration.

✈️ Flights

Fly into Noi Bai International Airport (HAN) in Hanoi. Book early for the best fares — it's a long-haul trip for most of us. Plan to arrive at least a day before the cruise.

🧳 What to Pack

Wedding attire, comfortable clothes for cave & island visits, swimwear, sturdy walking shoes, sunscreen, a light rain layer, and motion-sickness medicine. Bring any personal medications you need.

💵 Money

The currency is the Vietnamese Dong (VND). Cards work in cities; carry cash for markets and small vendors. ATMs are widely available. Onboard, you'll settle drinks, spa, and incidentals at check-out.

📱 Staying Connected

Grab a local eSIM or SIM card for cheap data, and use the Grab app for taxis. Note: Wi-Fi onboard is available in the restaurant but limited out in the bay — a lovely excuse to unplug.

📋 Good to Know

No outside food or drink onboard without the cruise's consent. Smoking only in designated outdoor areas — never in cabins. The full onboard schedule and dress code will be shared before we sail.
